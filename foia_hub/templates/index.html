openFOIA jump-starts your U.S. Freedom of Information Act request process by offering current contact information for all federal agencies.

What is FOIA?

The U.S. Freedom of Information Act (FOIA, for short) allows all people the right to request access to government records.

Under FOIA, government agencies are required to disclose requested information unless it falls under a specific set of exemptions or exclusions.

Learn more about FOIA

How does the process work?

  1. You (the requester) submit a FOIA request to the agency you think has the information you seek.
  2. In most cases, FOIA officials at that agency will confirm (via email) receipt of your request and begin looking for the records you requested.
  3. If your requested records are already publicly available, FOIA officials will usually let you know and point you in the right direction.
  4. As FOIA officials collect your requested records, they'll evaluate the material to determine what they can send to you.
  5. FOIA officials will send you the materials they're legally able to share. The records you receive may be in full, partial or redacted. In some cases, your request may be denied.
