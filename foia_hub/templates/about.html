About this Project

openFOIA aims to improve the FOIA request experience.

The Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) grants the public (everyone!) the right to access information from the Federal Government. The FOIA process requires members of the public to submit requests for information or records from a specific federal agency. If the requested information is able to be shared publicly, the agency will grant access to the requested records.

openFOIA is an online tool to guide users to up-to-date FOIA contact and request information across the federal government. It is a work in progress by 18F. The data we display is accurate to the best of our knowledge.

Getting better all the time

Our work is one of several initiatives currently in progress to modernize the Freedom of Information Act.

We're currently in the alpha stage of development, which means are site might not have every feature you're looking for.

Please let us know what's working and what you'd like to see in the future - we're actively improving openFOIA, and we base our improvements on feedback from users like you! Email your suggestions to 18f-foia@gsa.gov, or visit our issue tracker here.

Developers should check out the developer resources page.

Current Features

  • Up-to-date contact information

    openFOIA consolidates the contact information for all federal agencies’ and components’ FOIA offices. It’s easy to search, and it provides links to each agency’s online FOIA request form (when available), allowing users to start their requests more quickly.

    • Search

      It’s not always easy navigating the government’s hierarchy to find where to submit a FOIA request. openFOIA provides different search options to help requesters navigate to the appropriate agency or office within.

    • Keyword Search

      openFOIA lets you search by keyword, which is helpful if you’re not sure which agency to contact. For example, a search for 'benzene' brings up the Environmental Protection Agency, and a search for 'criminal laws' brings up the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives.

    • Acronym Search

      Some agencies are better known by their acronyms. openFOIA allows users to search by acronym instead of full agency (or component) title. For example, a search for 'FBI' brings up the Federal Bureau of Investigation.

  • FOIA Libraries

    Many agencies provide commonly requested responsive documents in online libraries or reading rooms. openFOIA links to these libraries, letting users see if the information they want is already available.
