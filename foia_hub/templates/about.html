FOIA Hub aims to improve the FOIA request experience.

The Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) grants the public (everyone!) the right to access information from the federal government. The FOIA process requires the public to submit a request for information or records within a specific federal agency.

FOIA Hub is an online tool to guide users to up-to-date FOIA contact and request information across the federal government. It is a work in progress by 18F.

Features

Up-to-date contact information

FOIA Hub provides a single, easily searchable source for contact information for FOIA offices within various agencies and components of the United States government.

Links are provided to an agency's online FOIA request form where available.

Search

Navigating the federal government hierarchy to determine where to submit a FOIA request is not easy.

FOIA Hub provides a number of search options to help requesters navigate to the appropriate agency or office within.

Keyword Search

You can search by various keywords. For example, a search for 'benzene' brings up the Environmental Protection Agency. Or a search for 'criminal laws' brings up the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives.

Acronym Search

Some agencies are better known by their acronym. You can search for an agency by acronym. 'FBI' brings up the Federal Bureau of Investigation.

FOIA Libraries

Many agencies provide oft-requested responsive documents in online libraries, or "reading rooms". It might help a potential requester to check those libraries before submitting a request.
